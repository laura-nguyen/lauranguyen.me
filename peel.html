High School Co-op 2017

District Program Arts Facilitator (High School Co-op)

Summary Reflection Promotion Material Art Exhibit


Summary

My first marketing experience consisted of launching a community based art exhibit as a District Program Arts Facilitator for the Peel District School Board. During the duration of my high school co-op, I was given exposure to the various problems that students with developmental disabilities face in terms of obtaining employment. First, I joined a social innovation lab, from Inspired Initiatives, to analyze current trends and the stigma in students with special needs. Then, I conducted interviews with various employers, teaching staff, and families in order to answer the question of "How can these students turn their individual skills into income opportunities"? As a facilitator, I realized that these certain students who have an unmeasurable talent in art, can have entrepreneurial pursuits by having an art exhibit. I communicated with venues, and teaching staff in order to manage the timing for the art exhibit and figure out what resources are needed in order to execute the event successfully. Furthermore, I promoted the art exhibit and the District Art Program itself by managing a website, social media accounts, and designing posters. After four months of organizing and planning, the art exhibit attracted large turnout and the student artists made $900 in sales. Taking such a huge leadership role, I couldn’t have accomplish this project with the help of the teaching staff, employers, and mentors. This experience is something that I will always cherish.

Reflection

It wasn't a traditional co-op job as in working at a retail store or being in an office. I was a bit hesitant because I had to get out of my comfort zone for this job. I had uncertainties whether if I made the right decision on choosing this job or if it would be better off to work at somewhere else. However, all my uncertainties went away when I got to meet the people who I would work with for the duration of the semester. Everyone was friendly and approachable and I knew for a fact that we would be a great team. ​

Brainstorming ideas on how youth with developmental disabilities can turn their skills into self-income opportunities.

One of the successes I had while being in this job was launching an e-commerce for artists with developmental disabilities. The community artists that I've been communicating via email agreed to have their art pieces be showcased on the website. My goal for the website was to have people see the creative talents from these youth. One of the greatest function on the website is that people are able to buy artwork through this website, and they can contact the artists' families directly in order to accomplish that. Another success that I've experienced is that the TLK DP Art Exhibit came into fruition. After several months of through planning and organizing, I was able to make this art exhibit happen. The atmosphere during the event was full of energy and I heard nothing but positive thoughts coming from individuals. It's amazing to see people support their community because when people unite, they will move forward. ​ ​

Communicating with teaching staff, families, and community partnerships via email to successfully launch TL Kennedy's DP Art Exhibit.

Now that my co-op placement is over, I've change drastically in terms of my mindset and attitude towards everything around me. I am sad that it's over, however I'm happy that I got the experience and that will give me a positive impact towards my future plans. I've learned a lot in terms of how to improve myself and how I can use big ideas to create impact in the community. I feel more proficient in my skills and I'm looking forward to improving them even more. This co-op reassures me that marketing is something that I would like to do as a career, and my workplace needs to include collaborating with people, and having meaningful work.
